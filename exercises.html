Exercise Library

Learning proper form is one of the most important parts of training. Good technique helps you build muscle more effectively, avoid injury, and get the most out of every rep. Browse the exercises below to learn how each movement works and what muscles it targets.

Common Exercises

Barbell Back Squat

Muscles Targeted: Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings, Core

The barbell back squat is a compound lower body exercise and one of the best movements for building overall leg strength and size. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, bar resting on your upper traps. Brace your core, push your knees out, and lower until your thighs are parallel to the floor. Drive through your heels to return to the starting position.

Bench Press

Muscles Targeted: Chest, Triceps, Front Deltoids

The bench press is the most popular upper body pushing exercise for building chest strength and mass. Lie flat on a bench, grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width, and lower the bar to your mid-chest with control. Press the bar back up to full arm extension. Keep your feet flat on the floor and your back slightly arched throughout the movement.

Deadlift

Muscles Targeted: Hamstrings, Glutes, Lower Back, Traps, Core

The deadlift is a full body compound movement that builds posterior chain strength from the floor up. Stand with feet hip-width apart, bar over your mid-foot. Hinge at the hips, grip the bar just outside your legs, and drive through the floor while keeping your back flat and chest up. Lock out at the top by squeezing your glutes, then lower the bar back down with control.

Overhead Press

Muscles Targeted: Shoulders, Triceps, Upper Chest, Core

The overhead press builds strong and broad shoulders while also challenging your core stability. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, bar resting on your front deltoids with a grip just outside shoulder-width. Press the bar straight up overhead until your arms are fully extended, then lower it back to the starting position with control. Keep your core tight and avoid leaning back excessively.

Pull-Up

Muscles Targeted: Lats, Biceps, Rear Deltoids, Core

Pull-ups are one of the best bodyweight exercises for building a wide and strong back. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width with palms facing away from you. Hang with arms fully extended, then pull yourself up until your chin clears the bar. Lower yourself back down slowly and with control. If you are new to pull-ups, use an assisted pull-up machine or resistance band to build up your strength.

Barbell Row

Muscles Targeted: Lats, Rhomboids, Traps, Biceps

The barbell row is a foundational back exercise that builds thickness and strength across the entire upper back. Hinge at the hips with a flat back, grip the bar shoulder-width apart, and pull the bar toward your lower chest or upper abdomen. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement, then lower the bar back down with control.
