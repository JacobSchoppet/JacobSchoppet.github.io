Choose Your Workout Split

A workout split is how you divide your training across the week. The right split depends on how many days you can commit to the gym. Browse the options below and find the plan that fits your schedule and experience level.

Split Comparison Chart

Use this table to quickly compare each split option and decide which one works best for you.

Days Per Week Split Type Muscle Groups Trained Per Session Best For
2 Days Full Body All major muscle groups each session Beginners, busy schedules
3 Days Full Body All major muscle groups each session Beginners to intermediate lifters
4 Days Upper / Lower Upper body days and lower body days alternated Intermediate lifters
5 Days Push / Pull / Legs Push muscles, pull muscles, and legs on separate days Intermediate to advanced lifters
6 Days Push / Pull / Legs (x2) Full PPL cycle repeated twice per week Advanced lifters

2-Day Split: Full Body

The 2-day full body split is perfect for people with very limited time or those just starting out. Each session targets all major muscle groups so you still make progress even with a minimal schedule. Rest days between sessions allow for full recovery.

Sample Schedule

Day 1: Squats, Bench Press, Bent-Over Row, Overhead Press, Plank

Day 2: Deadlifts, Incline Press, Pull-Ups, Lunges, Core Work

3-Day Split: Full Body

Training three days per week with a full body approach is one of the most well-researched and effective routines for building strength and muscle. You hit each muscle group three times per week, which is ideal for beginners and intermediate lifters alike.

Sample Schedule

Day 1 (Monday): Squat, Bench Press, Barbell Row

Day 2 (Wednesday): Squat, Overhead Press, Deadlift

Day 3 (Friday): Squat, Bench Press, Barbell Row

4-Day Split: Upper / Lower

The upper/lower split divides your training into two upper body days and two lower body days per week. This allows for higher training volume per muscle group while still allowing adequate recovery time. It is a great step up from full body training.

Sample Schedule

Day 1 (Monday): Upper Body — Bench Press, Rows, Overhead Press, Curls, Triceps

Day 2 (Tuesday): Lower Body — Squats, Romanian Deadlifts, Leg Press, Calf Raises

Day 3 (Thursday): Upper Body — Incline Press, Pull-Ups, Lateral Raises, Face Pulls

Day 4 (Friday): Lower Body — Deadlifts, Leg Curl, Walking Lunges, Core Work

5-Day Split: Push / Pull / Legs

Push/Pull/Legs is one of the most popular training splits among intermediate and advanced lifters. Push days train chest, shoulders, and triceps. Pull days train back and biceps. Leg days train quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. Over five days you complete one full cycle with two rest days.

Sample Schedule

Day 1: Push — Bench Press, Overhead Press, Lateral Raises, Triceps Pushdown

Day 2: Pull — Deadlift, Pull-Ups, Barbell Row, Bicep Curls

Day 3: Legs — Squat, Leg Press, Romanian Deadlift, Calf Raises

Day 4: Push — Incline Press, Cable Fly, Overhead Press, Skull Crushers

Day 5: Pull — Rack Pull, Seated Row, Face Pulls, Hammer Curls

6-Day Split: Push / Pull / Legs x2

The 6-day PPL split runs the full push/pull/legs cycle twice per week. This gives each muscle group two training sessions per week with high volume, making it ideal for advanced lifters focused on maximizing muscle growth. Only one rest day per week means you need to be fully committed to recovery, sleep, and nutrition.

Sample Schedule

Day 1: Push A — Bench Press, Overhead Press, Lateral Raises, Triceps

Day 2: Pull A — Deadlift, Pull-Ups, Barbell Row, Bicep Curls

Day 3: Legs A — Squat, Leg Press, Romanian Deadlift, Calf Raises

Day 4: Push B — Incline Press, Cable Fly, Arnold Press, Skull Crushers

Day 5: Pull B — Rack Pull, Seated Row, Face Pulls, Hammer Curls

Day 6: Legs B — Front Squat, Leg Curl, Walking Lunges, Core Work
